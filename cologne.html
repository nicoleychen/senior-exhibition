Cologne

I take a final glance at the window, reassuring myself that he really isn’t here, she’s fine, before turning my back to the house. A cold breeze whistles through the air, lifting some of the leaves from their place on the lawn. They land on the pavement behind me and continue to scrape their way along.

The wind makes me shiver and for a moment I can feel him, almost see him. The air smells like his damp breath and burning cologne and it’s wrapping around my skin, sending tingles down every inch of my spine. I can’t breathe without breathing him so now my heart is racing to keep me alive but I can’t help to think that maybe if I died I wouldn’t have to feel him anymore.

The illusion is over in a few seconds but it’s already too late; my mind is aching to be out of here, to be anywhere but here. My body obeys and I am moving away from the house, and more importantly, him.

But before I can take more than a few steps, I hear the crack of the window opening. I will myself to keep walking, to not turn around.

Don’t look at her. I can hear his wife humming a tune and the faint thumps as she paces. Don’t.

It’s a quiet street; if I could just get out...
